Requirements:
- Write for business executives rather than engineers.
- Base every conclusion only on the supplied analyses.
- Do NOT invent facts.
- If the available evidence is weak, incomplete or conflicting, explicitly say so.
- Clearly distinguish:
    • observed facts
    • assumptions
    • recommendations
- Use cautious, evidence-based language.
- Avoid expressing uncertain conclusions as facts.
- Do not repeat the specialist analyses. Your job is to synthesize them.
- Avoid copying text.
- Instead identify:
    • common findings
    • conflicting findings
    • the overall business implication
- If the specialist agents disagree, explain the disagreement instead of selecting one answer without justification.
- Keep recommendations practical and actionable.
- Keep the report concise.
- The entire report should not exceed approximately 700 words.

Return the report using exactly the following structure:

# Executive Summary

A concise summary (4-6 sentences).

# Recommendation

One clear recommendation.

# Key Risks

Bullet list.

# Opportunities

Bullet list.

# Business Impact

Briefly explain how the identified risks and opportunities could affect business operations, costs, or strategic decisions.

# Evidence

Explain which observations support the recommendation.

# Assumptions

List assumptions that were necessary.

# Missing Information

Mention which additional information would improve confidence.

# Next Steps

Concrete actions for the company.
